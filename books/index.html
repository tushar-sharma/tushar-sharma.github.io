Books on my shelf

These are some of books, other miscellaneous writings, & courses that are currently on my shelf. Feel free to suggest me any book or literature that you feel is interesting.

Title Author Status Comments
Great Expectation Charles Dickens
Read

This was my first novel of Charles Dickens. The novel begins its pace with the innocence of Pip, an orphan and is inadvertent kindness towards a convict which cascades into a journey of both fortune & misfortune. The rustic charm of Dickens is apparent throughtout the novel. It's humorous at times, often grim but retains the literary prowess of Dickens. If you're a fan of classics, it's a fascinating read.

Man's Search for Meaning Viktor Frankl
Read

Viktor Frankl is a Holocaust survivor and a neurologist and psychiatrist. In his memoir he narrates his life in Nazi concentrated camps. It's an interesting read on how he proposes to find meaning in life amidst hostile environment.

Anna Karenina Leo Tolstoy
Read

It's a classic, acclaimed as world's greatest novel though was a tedious read. Tolstoy's imagination was ravishing. It seems Tolstoy himself broods over life & convication through Lenin. Anna herself is a sophisticated character as she follows her journey from grace & beauty to the tragic path of passions. It's a must read novel once in a life.

Read

He was the first Vice President of independent India & her most distinguished twentieth-century scholars of comparative religion and philosophy. Read More
Structural Realism: The Best of Both Worlds? John Worrall
Read

Realism and antirealism are two sides of philosophical debate behind whole basis of accepting scientific theories. Realism relies on empirical success of theories as a proof that theories are somewhat latched to the 'blueprint of the universe'. However antirealism, argues how successful theories of the past have been proven false. A good introduction to structural realism, published in journal 'Dialectica', Worrall argues for a middle ground between realism and antirealism.
