## Double IPA

I had a beer called a Double IPA (Imperial IPA), which is a stronger version of the India Pale Ale. It typically has more hops and a higher alcohol content.

**What are hops?**
Hops are the flowers of the hop plant *Humulus lupulus*. They are used primarily as a bittering, flavoring, and stability agent in beer. In the past, hops acted as a natural preservative, which was crucial for long voyages.

**History of IPA**
There is a common misconception that IPA was "invented in India." In reality, it was developed in England specifically for the India trade during the era of the East India Company. The stronger, highly-hopped pale ale survived the long sea journey from England to India much better than other styles. This is why it became known as India Pale Ale.

## North Carolina Roadside Assistance: *47 vs. Insurance

If you ever get stuck on the road in North Carolina, you can dial **\*47** (or **\*HP**) from your mobile phone. This connects you to the North Carolina State Highway Patrol and their **Incident Management Assistance Patrol (IMAP)**.

**Is it different from GEICO or other support?**
Yes. While you might see the GEICO logo on these state trucks (they sponsor the program), **\*47 is a free state-funded service** for anyone driving in NC. In contrast, roadside assistance from companies like GEICO or AAA is a paid service.

**Should this be your first point of contact?**
It depends on where you are:

*   **On Major Highways/Interstates:** **Yes, call \*47 first.** It is free and designed specifically for major routes like I-40, I-77, or I-85. They can help with flat tires, jump-starts, or a small amount of fuel to get you moving.
*   **On Local Streets/Residential Areas:** **No, call your insurance (GEICO/AAA) instead.** The free state patrol trucks do not operate on local city streets. If you call \*47 from a local street for a minor issue, the dispatcher will likely refer you to a private towing company or suggest you call your insurance.

**Summary:**
*   **Use \*47 if:** You have a minor issue on a **major highway**. It's the cheapest (free!) and fastest option.
*   **Use your insurance (GEICO/AAA) if:** You are on a **local street**, you need a tow to a specific mechanic, or you are locked out of your car.

In short: **\*47** for the highway, **Insurance** for everywhere else.
