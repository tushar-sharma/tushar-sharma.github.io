## Double IPA

I had a beer called a Double IPA (Imperial IPA), which is a stronger version of the India Pale Ale. It typically has more hops and a higher alcohol content.

**What are hops?**
Hops are the flowers of the hop plant *Humulus lupulus*. They are used primarily as a bittering, flavoring, and stability agent in beer. In the past, hops acted as a natural preservative, which was crucial for long voyages.

**History of IPA**
There is a common misconception that IPA was "invented in India." In reality, it was developed in England specifically for the India trade during the era of the East India Company. The stronger, highly-hopped pale ale survived the long sea journey from England to India much better than other styles. This is why it became known as India Pale Ale.
